The Digital Pulse of Boston

A story of electricity, architecture, and the invisible footprints of a city.

This interactive energy dashboard transforms over 100,000 data points from Boston's open datasets into a cinematic narrative. By decoding 15-minute electricity intervals, it reveals the seasonal, weekly, and hourly "digital pulse" of one of the city's most iconic public structures.

A City's Silent Rhythm

Behind the heavy concrete walls of Boston City Hall, a silent conversation is happening. Every 15 minutes, the building speaks in numbers kilowatts of demand that trace the arrival of workers, the humming of air conditioners, and the deep breaths of a structure that never truly sleeps.

As Boston moves toward a greener future, these numbers are more than just utility costs. They are a map of our habits, showing us where we are efficient and where we leave the lights on for no one.

Patterns in the Dark

By decoding over 100,000 data points from the City's open records, we can see the building's "heartbeat." The patterns are not just random spikes; they tell the story of the seasons and the workweek.

The Seasonal Shift

Seasonality: This chart visualizes how energy demand fluctuates throughout the year. The significant peaks in July and August represent the building's massive cooling requirements during Boston's humid summers. A smaller secondary rise in the winter months reflects the energy needed to keep systems running against the cold.

The Working Life

The Workweek: By averaging demand across the days of the week, we see the building's operational pulse. The high plateau from Monday to Friday matches standard city hall hours, while the noticeable dip on Saturday and Sunday shows the building entering a lower-power state when staff are away.

24 Hours in the Concrete Giant

The Daily Heartbeat: This 24-hour profile is an average of all days. You can see the building "waking up" sharply at 6:00 AM as lights and systems activate. Power peaks around 2:00 PM. Crucially, notice that the "Baseline" at 3:00 AM never hits zero; this is the constant energy cost of keeping a modern city hall "alive."

Toward a Transparent Future

Data is the first step toward change. By making the invisible energy of our public buildings visible, we empower every citizen to ask: "Can we do better?" Boston City Hall is a monument of the past, but its energy data is a blueprint for our future.
